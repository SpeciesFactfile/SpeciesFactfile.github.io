Smutsia temminckii.

The African Ground Pangolin (Smutsia temminckii) is a ground dwelling creature that resides in woodland/Savannah habitats.

STATUS

The African Ground Pangolin is endangered due to an increase in poaching for its scales. The scales are taken to Asian countries where they are used in ' Traditional Chinese Medecine'. Scientific studies have proven the medecine useless.

DIET

The African Ground Pangolin's diet solely consists of ants and termites making them myrmecophagous.




Megaptera novaeangliae.

The Humpback Whale or Megaptera novaeangliae is a marine species that migrate long distances across open ocean every year to give birth to young in warm, predator free waters like the Hawaiian Islands.

STATUS

The Humpback Whale is currently least concerned after a ban came into force banning Whale Hunting in 1985.

DIET

The Humpback Whale's diet consists of small crustaceans like krill and fish. They employ several clever feeding strategies like bubble-net fishing and trap-feeding.



LOXODONTA AFRICANA

The African Bush Elephant or Loxodonta Africana, is the largest living land animal and is one of the big 5.

African Elephants are matriarchal meaning they are lead by one experienced female.

STATUS

The African Bush Elephant is currently endangered due to an increase in poaching for its tusks, which are made out of ivory. The trafficked ivory is then shipped to Asia where wealthy buyers are waiting.

DIET

The African Bush Elephant's diet mainly consists of grasses,fruits and bark.
